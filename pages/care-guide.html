1.Light Requirements


Different plants have varying light needs. Most tropical houseplants, like Monsteras, Pothos, and Peace Lilies, thrive in bright, indirect light, meaning they should be placed near a window where sunlight is filtered through a curtain or shade. Plants like cacti and succulents require direct sunlight and should be positioned on a sunny windowsill or in an area that receives at least 6 hours of sun per day. On the other hand, some plants, such as ZZ plants, Snake plants, and ferns, can adapt to low-light conditions, making them ideal for offices or rooms with limited natural light. If natural light is insufficient, grow lights can be used to supplement plant growth.
Bright, indirect light: Most indoor plants (e.g., Monstera, Pothos, Peace Lily) thrive in filtered sunlight.
Direct sunlight: Succulents, cacti, and some flowering plants need direct sun exposure.
Low light: ZZ plants, Snake plants, and ferns can tolerate low-light conditions.

4.Pruning and Maintenance


Regular maintenance keeps plants looking their best and encourages healthy growth. Prune dead or yellowing leaves to prevent disease and improve plant appearance. Some plants, such as Pothos and Philodendrons, benefit from trimming leggy stems to promote bushier growth. Rotating plants every few weeks ensures even light exposure, preventing them from leaning toward one direction. Dusting leaves with a damp cloth removes dirt and allows for better photosynthesis. Some plants, like Peace Lilies, benefit from occasional showers to wash off dust and keep leaves hydrated.
Trim dead leaves & flowers: Encourages new growth and prevents disease.
Rotate plants: Ensures even growth by exposing all sides to light.
Dust leaves: Clean with a damp cloth to improve photosynthesis.
